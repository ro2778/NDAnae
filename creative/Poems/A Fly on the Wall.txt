A fly on the wall

The other day a fly flew into the operating theatre where the patient was under drapes, with their elbow stuck out and bent unnaturally, the tissue had been peeled back and the bones exposed. Two surgeons in full body "space suits" were operating on the elbow. The patient under the drapes wasn't moving, tubes and wires ran out from under the blue sheets and fed into a machine, humming and beeping in the corner. There were at least a dozen people in theatre, performing their various tasks.

I watched the fly, it had landed on the canopy, which itself was actually a sophisticated ventilation system used to rapidly recycle air and keep the bugs out. The fly seemed to be looking down, wondering what these humans had created as the rolled magazine crushed its tiny mind.

As its dead body dropped to the floor, I looked at the room differently and thought, yeah it's pretty cool what we can do when we work together and want to achieve something. I considered, if that fly entered my house and saw me lying in bed, then it could probably understand that, it probably sleeps as well; there lies another sleeping animal. But it flew into an operating theatre, that fly has witnessed something most other flies will never see, like those incinerated at liftoff, it witnessed the limits of human culture. That's special, perhaps even worth dying for.

TLDR:

A fly on the wall,

Witness to the surgery,

Dies nonetheless. Gone.
